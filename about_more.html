I grew up in Duluth, Minnesota, and then attended college and law school in the Twin Cities.

I came to Chicago in 2012 for a job at a non-profit.

I did a mix of legal research, project management, and process improvement. It was a great experience working on very challenging projects with people across the country, and I looked forward to doing similar work in my next job, that was until I started programming...

In the beginning, I didn't plan on becoming a full-time developer. I just wanted to make my own apps. I thought to myself, "I've unclogged sinks and replaced brake pads with Google searches and Youtube videos. I should be able to make an app. How hard can it be?"

Without any clear plans in mind, I started coding and quickly fell in love with it. I had already enjoyed creative writing, but developing software expressed a new form of creativity for me. So I spent countless hours alone in front of the computer after my wife went to bed.

Now, I'm at Dev Bootcamp, weaving together the strands of programming knowledge I've gathered independently, and finalizing my transition into the tech industry.

While Dev Bootcamp's curriculum seems to focus pretty heavily on Ruby and the Rails framework, I am also developing knowledge and skills with JavaScript web frameworks.

Also, I am still determined to realize my intial goal of making cool apps for my phone, so I study Android and Java in my "spare time". Follow me on Twitter and I'll let you know when I make something cool.

When I'm not hustling to prepare for my first job in the tech industry, I'm often with my wife as we prepare for our first baby. Our small apartment keeps getting smaller as we add a stroller and bassinet and toys and clothes.
